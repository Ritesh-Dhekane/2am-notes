## Q1. Define Research. Explain the steps in the Research Process. [10 Marks]

**Research** is a systematic and scientific search for pertinent information on a specific topic.

### Steps in Research Process:
1. **Defining the Research Problem**: Formulating a clear, concise statement of the problem.
2. **Review of Literature**: Studying existing research, books, and journals to understand the context.
3. **Formulating Hypothesis**: Creating a tentative assumption to test.
4. **Preparing Research Design**: The blueprint of the research (Sampling, data tools, etc.).
5. **Data Collection**: Gathering information via Primary (Surveys) or Secondary (Reports) sources.
6. **Data Analysis**: Using statistical tools (SPSS, R) to analyze the gathered data.
7. **Interpretation and Report Writing**: Drawing conclusions and documenting the findings.

## Q2. Differentiate between Probability and Non-Probability Sampling. [5 Marks]

| Probability Sampling | Non-Probability Sampling |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Selection** | Random selection of participants. | Non-random selection based on convenience. |
| **Bias** | Low bias, highly representative. | High chance of bias, less representative. |
| **Inference** | Results can be generalized to the population. | Results are specific to the sample. |
| **Techniques** | Simple Random, Stratified, Cluster. | Convenience, Quota, Snowball. |

## Q3. Explain the different types of Data Collection methods. [5 Marks]

1. **Primary Data**:
   - Collected for the first time by the researcher.
   - *Methods*: Questionnaire, Personal Interview, Observation.
2. **Secondary Data**:
   - Already collected by someone else.
   - *Methods*: Government publications, Websites, Internal Records, Journals.
