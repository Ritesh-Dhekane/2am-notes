## Q1. What are the different types of Research? Explain in detail. [10 Marks]

1. **Descriptive Research**: Describes the state of affairs as it exists (Surveys, fact-finding).
2. **Analytical Research**: Uses facts or information already available to make a critical evaluation.
3. **Applied Research**: Aims at finding a solution for an immediate problem facing a society or organization.
4. **Fundamental Research**: Concerned with generalizations and with the formulation of a theory.
5. **Quantitative Research**: Based on the measurement of quantity or amount.
6. **Qualitative Research**: Concerned with qualitative phenomena (e.g., human behavior).

## Q2. Explain the difference between Primary and Secondary data. [5 Marks]

| Feature | Primary Data | Secondary Data |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Origin** | Collected by the researcher for the first time. | Already exists (collected by others). |
| **Originality** | It is original and unique. | It is not original. |
| **Source** | Surveys, Interviews, Observations. | Books, Journals, Reports, Websites. |
| **Cost** | More expensive and time-consuming. | Cheaper and faster to obtain. |

## Q3. Write a short note on Bibliography and its importance. [5 Marks]

A **Bibliography** is a list of all the sources (books, journals, websites) that were consulted or cited during the research process.
- **Importance**:
  - Gives credit to original authors.
  - Allows readers to verify the information.
  - Demonstrates the depth of the research.
  - Prevents plagiarism.
