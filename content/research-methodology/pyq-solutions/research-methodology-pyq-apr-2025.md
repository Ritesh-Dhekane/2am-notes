## Question 1

### (a) Describe research process with suitable diagram in detail. [5]

#### Answer

## Research Process

Research process is a systematic sequence of steps used to solve a research problem scientifically and effectively.

### Steps in Research Process

1. **Identification of Research Problem**

   * Select and define the problem clearly.
   * Determine scope and objectives.

2. **Literature Review**

   * Study books, journals, research papers, and previous work.
   * Understand existing knowledge and research gap.

3. **Formulation of Research Questions/Objectives**

   * Define purpose of research.
   * Prepare objectives and questions.

4. **Research Design**

   * Select suitable research design and methodology.
   * Decide tools and techniques.

5. **Data Collection**

   * Collect primary or secondary data.
   * Use surveys, interviews, questionnaires, etc.

6. **Sampling**

   * Select representative sample from population.

7. **Data Analysis**

   * Analyze data using statistical methods.
   * Interpret findings.

8. **Conclusion**

   * Summarize findings and results.

9. **Report Writing**

   * Prepare final research report systematically.

### Diagram of Research Process

```text
Identify Problem
       ↓
Literature Review
       ↓
Research Objectives
       ↓
Research Design
       ↓
Data Collection
       ↓
Sampling
       ↓
Data Analysis
       ↓
Conclusion
       ↓
Report Writing
```

> Important Note: Research process helps in conducting organized and reliable research.

### (a) Explain Probability and Non-probability Sampling methods with suitable examples. [10]

#### Answer

# Sampling

Sampling is the process of selecting a subset from a population for research study.

---

# 1. Probability Sampling

In probability sampling, every member has equal chance of selection.

## Types

### a) Simple Random Sampling

* Random selection using lottery method.

#### Example

* Selecting 50 students randomly.

---

### b) Systematic Sampling

* Select every kth element.

#### Example

* Every 10th customer in a mall.

---

### c) Stratified Sampling

* Population divided into groups.

#### Example

* Students selected from different departments.

---

### d) Cluster Sampling

* Population divided into clusters.

#### Example

* Selecting one college from many colleges.

---

## Advantages

* Unbiased results
* Reliable and accurate
* Suitable for statistical analysis

## Disadvantages

* Time-consuming
* Costly
* Requires population list

---

# 2. Non-Probability Sampling

Selection depends on convenience or judgment.

## Types

### a) Convenience Sampling

* Easily available participants selected.

#### Example

* Surveying nearby students.

---

### b) Judgment Sampling

* Participants selected based on expertise.

#### Example

* Selecting experienced software developers.

---

### c) Quota Sampling

* Fixed number selected from categories.

#### Example

* Selecting equal male and female students.

---

### d) Snowball Sampling

* Existing participants refer others.

#### Example

* Research on cybercrime groups.

---

## Advantages

* Quick and simple
* Low cost
* Useful for exploratory research

## Disadvantages

* High bias
* Less reliable
* Poor generalization

### (a) Discuss Chi-square test.

From the following data, find out whether there is any relationship between Gender (Male/Female) & Colour Preferences: [10]

| Colour | Males | Females | Total |
| ------ | ----- | ------- | ----- |
| Pink   | 10    | 40      | 50    |
| Black  | 70    | 30      | 100   |
| Yellow | 30    | 20      | 50    |
| Total  | 110   | 90      | 200   |

(Consider χ² value at 0.05 significance level and degree of freedom 2 = 5.99)

#### Answer

# Chi-Square Test

Chi-Square (χ²) test is a statistical test used to determine whether there is a significant relationship between two categorical variables.

## Formula

[
\chi^2 = \sum \frac{(O-E)^2}{E}
]

Where:

* O = Observed frequency
* E = Expected frequency

---

# Step 1: Hypothesis Formulation

## Null Hypothesis (H₀)

There is no relationship between Gender and Colour Preference.

## Alternate Hypothesis (H₁)

There is relationship between Gender and Colour Preference.

---

# Step 2: Expected Frequencies

## Formula

[
E = \frac{(Row\ Total)(Column\ Total)}{Grand\ Total}
]

---

## Expected Frequency Table

| Colour | Male Expected | Female Expected |
| ------ | ------------- | --------------- |
| Pink   | 27.5          | 22.5            |
| Black  | 55            | 45              |
| Yellow | 27.5          | 22.5            |

---

# Step 3: Chi-Square Calculation

| Cell          | O  | E    | (O−E)²/E |
| ------------- | -- | ---- | -------- |
| Pink Male     | 10 | 27.5 | 11.14    |
| Pink Female   | 40 | 22.5 | 13.61    |
| Black Male    | 70 | 55   | 4.09     |
| Black Female  | 30 | 45   | 5.00     |
| Yellow Male   | 30 | 27.5 | 0.23     |
| Yellow Female | 20 | 22.5 | 0.28     |

---

## Total χ² Value

[
\chi^2 = 34.35
]

---

# Step 4: Decision

* Calculated χ² value = 34.35
* Table χ² value = 5.99

Since:

[
34.35 > 5.99
]

* Reject Null Hypothesis (H₀)

---

# Conclusion

There is significant relationship between Gender and Colour Preference.

### (b) What is t-test? When is it used? [10]

Determine whether the average weight of sample of 20 mangoes is significantly different from population average weight of 70 gms.

Sample Mean = 70.55 gm
Sample Standard Deviation = 2.82 gm

(Consider t-value at 0.05 significance = 2.093)

#### Answer

# t-Test

t-test is a statistical test used to compare means and determine whether difference between sample mean and population mean is significant.

---

# Uses of t-Test

1. Used in hypothesis testing.
2. Used when sample size is small.
3. Compares sample and population means.
4. Useful in research experiments.

---

# Given Data

| Parameter              | Value |
| ---------------------- | ----- |
| Sample Size (n)        | 20    |
| Population Mean (μ)    | 70    |
| Sample Mean (x̄)       | 70.55 |
| Standard Deviation (s) | 2.82  |
| Table t-value          | 2.093 |

---

# Step 1: Hypothesis

## Null Hypothesis (H₀)

Average weight of mangoes is equal to 70 gm.

## Alternate Hypothesis (H₁)

Average weight of mangoes is significantly different from 70 gm.

---

# Step 2: Formula

[
t = \frac{\bar{x}-\mu}{s/\sqrt{n}}
]

---

# Step 3: Calculation

[
t = \frac{70.55-70}{2.82/\sqrt{20}}
]

[
\sqrt{20} = 4.472
]

[
2.82 / 4.472 = 0.63
]

[
0.55 / 0.63 = 0.87
]

---

# Calculated t-value

[
t = 0.87
]

---

# Step 4: Decision

* Calculated t-value = 0.87
* Table t-value = 2.093

Since:

[
0.87 < 2.093
]

* Accept Null Hypothesis (H₀)

---

# Conclusion

Average weight of sample mangoes is not significantly different from population average weight of 70 gm.
