## 1. Elaborate on Types of Research with examples.
**[APR-2025 | JAN-2026 | 10 Marks]**

1. **Descriptive Research:** Describing a situation or phenomenon (e.g., census, survey of shopping habits).
2. **Analytical Research:** Using facts or information already available to analyze and make evaluations (e.g., historical analysis).
3. **Applied Research:** Finding a solution for an immediate problem (e.g., solving a decrease in sales for a specific company).
4. **Fundamental (Basic) Research:** Generalizing and formulating a theory (e.g., research in pure mathematics).
5. **Empirical Research:** Based on experience or observation alone (e.g., testing the impact of a new drug).

---

## 2. What is a Hypothesis? Explain Null and Alternate Hypothesis.
**[APR-2025 | 10 Marks]**

A hypothesis is a tentative assumption made in order to draw out and test its logical or empirical consequences.

### Types of Hypothesis:
* **Null Hypothesis ($H_0$):** Assumes there is NO significant difference or relationship. It's what the researcher tries to disprove.
* **Alternate Hypothesis ($H_a$):** Assumes there IS a significant difference or relationship. It's the researcher's actual prediction.

---

## 3. Case Study: Formulate Hypothesis
**[APR-2025 | 5 Marks]**

**Situation:** Researcher wants to understand the impact of social media usage on children’s behavior.

* **Null Hypothesis ($H_0$):** There is no significant relationship between social media usage and changes in children's behavior.
* **Alternate Hypothesis ($H_a$):** Increased social media usage significantly leads to more aggressive/introverted behavior in children.

---

## 4. What are the key components of a Research Design?
**[APR-2025 | JAN-2026 | 5 Marks]**

A research design is the conceptual structure within which research is conducted.

1. **The Sampling Design:** Method of selecting items to be observed.
2. **The Observational Design:** Conditions under which observations are created.
3. **The Statistical Design:** How the data will be analyzed.
4. **The Operational Design:** The procedures by which the sampling, observational, and statistical designs are carried out.
