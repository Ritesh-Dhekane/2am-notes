## 1. Explain Probability and Non-Probability Sampling methods.
**[APR-2025 | 10 Marks]**

### Probability Sampling (Random)
Every member of the population has a known, non-zero chance of being selected.
1. **Simple Random Sampling:** Every item has an equal chance (e.g., lottery method).
2. **Systematic Sampling:** Selecting every $n^{th}$ item from a list.
3. **Stratified Sampling:** Dividing population into groups (strata) and sampling from each.
4. **Cluster Sampling:** Dividing population into clusters and selecting entire clusters randomly.

### Non-Probability Sampling (Non-Random)
Selection is based on the researcher's judgment or convenience.
1. **Convenience Sampling:** Selecting participants who are easy to reach.
2. **Purposive (Judgmental) Sampling:** Selecting based on specific characteristics.
3. **Quota Sampling:** Selecting a specific number of people from different categories.
4. **Snowball Sampling:** Existing participants recruit future participants.

## 2. Explain Data Collection Methods.
**[JAN-2026 | 5 Marks]**

1. **Primary Data Collection:** Data collected for the first time by the researcher.
   * **Observation:** Watching and recording behavior.
   * **Interviews:** Face-to-face or telephonic conversations.
   * **Questionnaires:** Written set of questions.
2. **Secondary Data Collection:** Data that has already been collected by someone else.
   * **Internal Sources:** Company records, reports.
   * **External Sources:** Books, Journals, Websites, Government reports.

## 3. Numerical: Calculate Sample Size
**[JAN-2026 | 10 Marks]**

**Scenario:** Population ($N$) = 100,000, Confidence Level = 95% ($Z \approx 1.96$), Margin of Error ($e$) = 5% (0.05).

Using Slovin's Formula (simplified for exam purposes) or Cochran's Formula:
For large populations, the sample size ($n$) is approximately:
$n = \frac{Z^2 \cdot p \cdot (1-p)}{e^2}$
Assuming $p = 0.5$ (maximum variability):
$n = \frac{1.96^2 \cdot 0.5 \cdot 0.5}{0.05^2}$
$n = \frac{3.8416 \cdot 0.25}{0.0025} = \frac{0.9604}{0.0025} \approx 384$

**Result:** A sample size of approximately **384** is required.

## 4. Ethics in Research
**[JAN-2026 | 5 Marks]**

1. **Informed Consent:** Participants must be informed about the research and agree to participate.
2. **Confidentiality:** Protecting the identity and data of the participants.
3. **Anonymity:** Not collecting identifying information if not necessary.
4. **Objectivity:** Avoiding bias in data collection and interpretation.
5. **Plagiarism:** Giving proper credit to others' work and avoiding copying.
